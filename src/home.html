ABOUT ME

I was born and raised in South Bend, IN. I stayed in Indiana for college, and moved to Chicago shortly after graduation, where I've been for a little over 2 years.

I graduated from Indiana University Bloomington with a BS in neuroscience. Shortly after graduation, I began working full time with Dr. Lucy Godley at The University of Chicago, where I divided my time between managerial responsibilities and bioinformatic research. My research utilized Whole Exome Sequencing to characterize molecular progression towards leukemia, and identify novel risk alleles that predispose to hematopoietic disorders. Around the same time I began evening classes for my Masters program. I've enjoyed all of my classes throughout my program, but my favorites have been Web Development, Cloud Computing, and Distributed Systems.

To apply my skills outside of the classroom, I worked as a software development engineer intern at Amazon, where I worked on a customer-facing feature for Amazon Fashion. As a side project, I designed and developed the website for Runners-4-Readers, an awesome organization whose goal is to fight illiteracy.

In my free time I love getting outside and being active. Ideally this would mean hiking, but since this is hard to come by in Chicago, it usually means walking around and exploring all of Chicago's awesome neighborhoods. I'm an avid runner and biker - nothing beats a morning run or a ride along the lakefront trail! I also enjoy playing piano, guitar, and making pottery.

In addition to seeking a full-time software engineering position, I'm also open to free-lance work. If you have a project you think I can help with, feel free to reach out!
